Mentorship Matching System

A structured program that connects students with alumni and industry mentors based on skills, career interests, and availability — making mentorship easier to launch and easier to sustain.

Skill-Based Matching Match students and mentors by tools, roles, and goals.
Career Interests Align students with mentors in relevant industries and paths.
Availability Preferences Filter by meeting frequency and time commitments.
Structured Engagement Keep mentorship organized with goals and checkpoints.

Core Mentorship Features

Student Intake Profile

Students submit interests, career goals, target roles, and skill areas to help create a strong match.

Mentor Profile

Alumni and industry mentors list expertise, preferred mentoring topics, availability, and communication preferences.

Matching & Recommendations

The platform recommends pairings based on skills, interests, and availability—while allowing manual review.

Meeting Structure

Provide a simple cadence: goals, meeting notes, and suggested discussion prompts to keep mentorship consistent.

Mentorship Milestones

Track progress with milestones like resume review, mock interviews, career mapping, and networking checkpoints.

Program Oversight

CMIS administrators can monitor activity levels, match success, and overall mentorship engagement health.

How Matching Works

Matching is designed to be fair, scalable, and easy to manage:

  • Skill overlap: tools, technologies, and role alignment (ex: analytics, cloud, data, PM).
  • Career interests: industry, job functions, and long-term goals.
  • Availability: meeting frequency, preferred communication method, and time zones.
  • Preferences: mentoring topics (resume, interviewing, leadership, internship guidance).
  • Opt-in controls: both mentors and students can accept/decline suggested matches.
